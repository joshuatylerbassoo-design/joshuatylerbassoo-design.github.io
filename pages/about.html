Exhibit I

A Few Things I Hold to Be True

Not a biography — a short set of convictions, gathered the way a museum gathers its permanent collection. Each one earned, not borrowed.

I

One Foot in Front of the Other

Talent is common. Endurance is not. I have built every part of my life on the conviction that consistent, unglamorous effort outlasts and outperforms raw ability — every single time.

II

Work as a Form of Worship

I started working at fourteen, not out of necessity alone, but because I came to see labor itself as formative — a daily discipline that builds character long before it builds a résumé.

III

Service Before Self

Two years spent serving communities from Colorado Springs to Santos, Brazil, taught me that humility is not weakness — it is the price of admission to genuinely understanding another person's life.

IV

The Traditional Man

I believe in the quiet, unfashionable virtues — providing, protecting, showing up. I am drawn to the model of a man who leads his family not with volume, but with steadiness.

V

Sharpened in Community

I seek out people who share this mindset, because growth is rarely solitary. Iron sharpens iron — and I want to spend my life surrounded by people committed to bettering the world, one day at a time.

On Discipline

Hard work will beat talent every time talent doesn't work hard.

That sentence has quietly run underneath every job, every climb, every mile of trail, and every act of service I've taken on since I was fourteen years old. It's less a slogan than a compass.

"My goal is simple: to bless the people around me with the same hard work that was given to me at an early age."
— Joshua Bassoo
